## Content

Amy Eskridge, 34, died on June 11, 2022, in Huntsville, Alabama. She graduated from the University of Alabama in Huntsville with a double major in chemistry and biology, and held expertise in electrical engineering, chemistry, physics, and genetic engineering. She was the chair and president of the Institute for Exotic Science in Huntsville, which she co-founded. She had collaborated with her father, retired NASA engineer Richard Eskridge, on presentations about gravity-modification experiments through their company HoloChron Engineering.

Authorities ruled her death a self-inflicted gunshot wound. However, no detailed police reports, autopsy findings, or coroner's statements have been publicly released. Before her death, Eskridge had publicly stated that her life was in danger.

No official Huntsville Police Department press release was located. No institutional statement from the Institute for Exotic Science or other affiliated organizations was found.
