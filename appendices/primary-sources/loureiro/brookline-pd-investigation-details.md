## Content

Brookline Police Department released reports detailing the movements of shooter Claudio Manuel Neves Valente on December 15, 2025.

Valente pulled a gray Nissan Sentra into a parking spot on Babcock Street and lingered in the neighborhood. Around 8:00 PM, he began what police described as "preoperational surveillance," circling the immediate neighborhood multiple times. Police wrote that Valente was "gathering information of the neighborhood layout by his repeated presence and probable mapping of routes, including entry and exit points."

At 8:22 PM, a neighbor's Ring camera captured Valente wearing a yellow reflective safety vest and carrying a box with a barcode, disguised as a delivery driver. Around 8:30 PM, the doorbell rang several times. Loureiro's 12-year-old daughter opened the door and saw a man holding a package. Loureiro went to the door and was shot at least three times -- in the chest, abdomen, and thigh. Valente then drove away with headlights and taillights turned off.

Valente was later connected to the Brown University shooting two days prior (December 13, 2025, which killed two and injured nine). The Connecticut State Police Forensic Science Lab confirmed one of Valente's firearms matched the weapon used in Loureiro's murder.

Valente, a Portuguese national, died by self-inflicted gunshot wound in a New Hampshire storage unit on December 18, 2025. Both Valente and Loureiro had attended Instituto Superior Tecnico in Lisbon from 1995 to 2000.
