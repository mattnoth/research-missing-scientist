## Key Facts Extracted

- Full name: Nuno Loureiro (elsewhere given as Nuno Filipe Gomes Loureiro)
- Died: December 16, 2025, early morning, from gunshot wounds sustained hours earlier
- Age: 47
- Positions: Professor of Nuclear Science and Engineering; Professor of Physics; Herman Feshbach (1942) Professor of Physics; Director of MIT Plasma Science and Fusion Center (since 2024)
- Education: BS Physics, Instituto Superior Tecnico, Portugal; PhD Physics, Imperial College London (2005)
- Career: Princeton Plasma Physics Lab (postdoc, 2005-2007); UKAEA Culham Centre for Fusion Energy (2007); Institute for Plasmas and Nuclear Fusion, IST (2009-2016); MIT (2016-2025)
- Research: magnetized plasma dynamics, turbulence in fusion reactors, magnetic reconnection, astrophysical phenomena, quantum computing algorithms for plasma simulations
- Awards: NSF CAREER Award, APS Thomas H. Stix Award, APS Fellow, Presidential Early Career Award (2025), PAI Outstanding Professor Award (twice)
- Teaching: Course 22.612 (Intro to Plasma Physics), Course 22.615 (MHD Theory of Fusion Systems)
- Family: wife, children (number not specified in this source)

## Notable Quotes (paraphrased per source discipline)

- MIT President Sally Kornbluth expressed "great sadness" at the "tragic news" of Loureiro's death from gunshot wounds.
- Former department head Dennis Whyte called Loureiro "not only a brilliant scientist" but "a brilliant person."
- Physics department head Deepto Chakrabarty described Loureiro as a "champion for plasma physics" and "inspiring and caring mentor."
