## Excerpt (paraphrased with limited quotation)

Caltech's official memorial identifies Grillmair as Carl Grillmair (1959–2026), born in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, to parents Leo and Elfi Grillmair. He had two siblings: Elizabeth (b. 1960) and Walter (b. 1961, d. 1981). He was survived by his wife Louise.

His education: B.Sc. in astrophysics from the University of Calgary (1983), M.Sc. from the University of Victoria (1986), Ph.D. from the Australian National University (1993) with a thesis on globular cluster dynamics.

He joined IPAC in 1997. His roles included: member of the Spitzer Space Telescope instrument support team, deputy lead of the Infrared Spectrograph (IRS) team, quality-assurance scientist and pipeline operator at the NEOWISE Science Data Center, and instrument characterization specialist for NASA's Near-Earth Object Surveyor mission.

Research output: 147 peer-reviewed publications with 3 in preparation at the time of his death. Over 400 hours of telescope time as principal investigator (Hubble and Spitzer), and over 2,700 hours as co-investigator.

Key discovery: In 2007, he was lead author on a publication that first identified molecules in exoplanet atmospheres using Spitzer data. He also discovered dozens of stellar streams in the Milky Way.

Awards: 2011 NASA Exceptional Scientific Achievement Medal; multiple NASA Group Achievement Awards; 2006 Maclean's magazine honor roll; 1997 Caltech Gemini Fellowship.

IPAC executive director Tom Greene stated Grillmair was "part of IPAC's bedrock for many years." Colleague Sergio Fajardo-Acosta praised his methods as "truly detective work."

Personal life: avid pilot of small aircraft and gliders; maintained a home observatory; outdoor enthusiast.
