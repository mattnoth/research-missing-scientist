## Excerpt (paraphrased with limited quotation)

On March 3, 2026, the LA County Board of Supervisors adjourned its meeting in memory of Carl Grillmair. Fifth District Supervisor Kathryn Barger, who represents Pasadena, moved the adjournment.

The press release describes Grillmair as a Caltech astronomer who spent nearly three decades at the institute's Pasadena-based IPAC. It highlights his research detecting water in exoplanet atmospheres and his discovery of dozens of previously unknown stellar streams in the Milky Way.

The release notes his contributions to the Hubble Space Telescope and the Spitzer Space Telescope, and that NASA recognized him with the Exceptional Scientific Achievement Medal in 2011.

The statement refers to Grillmair as a "slain Antelope Valley astrophysicist," confirming the official characterization of his death as a homicide.
