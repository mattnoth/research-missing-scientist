## Summary

Email from Tom DeLonge (t.delonge@me.com) to John Podesta (john.podesta@gmail.com), subject line "General McCasland." DeLonge, identifying himself as founder of To The Stars Media, described a four-month working relationship with McCasland. He characterized McCasland as "very, very aware" of DeLonge's project while publicly maintaining a "skeptic" stance. DeLonge claimed McCasland had commanded the laboratory at Wright-Patterson AFB where Roswell materials were allegedly shipped, that McCasland "helped assemble my advisory team," and that McCasland was "a very important man." DeLonge also stated McCasland had received a four-hour briefing on the project several weeks prior.

## Key Excerpts (paraphrased per copyright discipline; one short quote)

- DeLonge stated he had been working with McCasland for four months and described him as very aware of the project.
- DeLonge referenced Wright-Patterson AFB and the claim that Roswell materials were shipped there, noting McCasland had commanded that laboratory.
- Direct quote (under 15 words): McCasland "helped assemble my advisory team."
- DeLonge called McCasland "a very important man" and said he had been briefed extensively.

## Provenance Notes

These emails were obtained through a data breach of John Podesta's Gmail account attributed to Fancy Bear (alleged Russian military intelligence, GRU Unit 26165). The documents have not been disputed as fabricated by any party. DeLonge later acknowledged the emails' existence but complained that WikiLeaks publication was taken out of context. McCasland has never publicly confirmed or denied the content of these emails.
