# White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt — Statement on Missing Scientists Review

- **Source URL:** https://www.foxnews.com/politics/white-house-reviewing-cases-missing-dead-scientists-possible-links-11th-person-identified
- **Access date:** 2026-04-20
- **Publisher/Author:** Fox News (quoting official White House statement)
- **Document type:** White House press briefing / official statement
- **Date of document:** 2026-04-17

## Content

On Wednesday, April 15, 2026, when asked by Fox News' Peter Doocy whether federal authorities were investigating potential connections among missing/dead scientists with classified access, Leavitt responded: "I've seen the report, Peter. I haven't spoken to our relevant agencies about it. I will certainly do that, and we'll get you an answer. If true, of course, that's definitely something I think this government and administration would deem worth looking into."

On Friday, April 17, 2026, Leavitt issued an official written statement via X/Twitter:

"In light of the recent and legitimate questions about these troubling cases, and President Trump's commitment to the truth, the White House is actively working with all relevant agencies and the FBI to holistically review all of the cases together and identify any potential commonalities that may exist. No stone will be unturned in this effort, and the White House will provide updates when we have them."

On the same day, President Trump told reporters on the White House South Lawn: "I hope it's random, but we're going to know in the next week and a half." He added: "Hopefully, I don't know, coincidence -- whatever you wanna call it -- but some of them were very important people, and we're going to look at it." Trump confirmed he had participated in a meeting regarding the cases, calling it "pretty serious stuff."
