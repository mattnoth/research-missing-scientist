## Garcia: KCNSC employment unverified
- **What we need:** Official confirmation (or denial) from KCNSC, Honeywell FM&T, NNSA, or APD that Steven Garcia was employed at or contracted to the Kansas City National Security Campus.
- **What we searched:** 15 web searches across news outlets, government databases, and institutional sites. Fetched NM DPS record, Newsweek, CBS, Fox, British Brief, Daily Wire, Newsweek FBI overview. No KCNSC or Honeywell statement found. Newsweek reported reaching out to KCNSC with no documented response.
- **Status:** Open. The KCNSC claim rests on a single anonymous source speaking to the Daily Mail. No independent verification has been published by any outlet.

## Garcia: No APD press release or Silver Alert located
- **What we need:** Official APD press release, media advisory, or Silver Alert for Steven Garcia's disappearance on August 28, 2025.
- **What we searched:** Searched for APD press releases, Silver Alerts, and police reports. Found NM DPS database listing (confirms APD as reporting agency) but no standalone APD document. APD statements in secondary reporting appear to come from media inquiries, not published releases.
- **Status:** Open. APD Records Division may have documents obtainable via records request (505-768-2020).

## Garcia: NNSA statement original text not located
- **What we need:** Full text, date, and medium of the NNSA statement acknowledging awareness of reports about missing employees.
- **What we searched:** Searched NNSA and KCNSC sites; found the statement only as quoted in secondary sources (Newsweek, CBS News). No standalone press release on nnsa.energy.gov or kcnsc.doe.gov.
- **Status:** Open.

## Garcia: No family statements found
- **What we need:** Any public statement from Garcia's family members about his disappearance.
- **What we searched:** All 15 searches and 11 page fetches. No family member is quoted in any located coverage.
- **Status:** Open.

## Foreign coverage: Daily Mail articles not directly accessible
- **What we need:** Direct URLs and text of Daily Mail articles on the missing scientists story. The Daily Mail is cited by RT, WION, and other foreign outlets as a key early source (particularly regarding intelligence-source claims), but no Daily Mail articles appeared in search results.
- **What we searched:** Multiple searches including site:dailymail.co.uk with various keyword combinations. No results returned.
- **Status:** Open. The Daily Mail may be poorly indexed by the search tool used, or articles may use different terminology. The Daily Mail's role as a narrative originator for the international story makes this a significant gap.
